Indicator

Model-Generated Definition:

Definition:

An indicator is a predicate such that matching sightings are evidence for a possible situation. Sightings matching an indicator suggests further study or action based on a course of action rule.

Subtypes of indicator define what is watched for: a pattern, a watch list or some combination of other indicators. Indicators may be restricted to (relevant only within) a specific scope.

Indicator Watchlist

Model-Generated Definition:

A kind of Indicator.

Definition: An indicator defined by a set of entities (which can be individuals' or situations) that should be watched for in a particular context. The members of the watch list are represented by "watches". When a watched individual is sighted, the indicator "fires", indicating instances of the indicated potential situations.

Threat

Model-Generated Definition:

A kind of Dangerous Event.

Definition: A threat is role of a situation that may lead to one or more related incidents or failures.
The threat consists of the existence of zero or more threat actors together with a set of one or more vulnerabilities. Thus, the threat of theft may result in an actual theft (attack), and threats correspond to attacks that are typically classified by attacker motivation (e.g., theft) as opposed to technique (e.g., spoofing). In some books and articles, the different but highly related terms â€œattackâ€ and â€œthreatâ€ are sometimes confounded by being used as synonyms [Firesmith 03, Tulloch 03].
